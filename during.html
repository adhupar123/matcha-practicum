During

This phase captures how matcha quality shifted from abstract knowledge to lived experience. Through tastings, preparation, and observation, each moment contributed to a deeper understanding of how quality is constructed, perceived, and refined over time.

Initial Comparative Tasting

Observation Analysis

Tasting multiple matcha side by side immediately revealed differences in bitterness, color, and aroma. What previously felt like abstract grading categories became sensory distinctions that could be directly experienced.

Recognizing Variation Within Grades

Observation Insight

Even within similar grades, noticeable variation emerged. This suggested that matcha quality exists on a spectrum rather than within rigid classifications, complicating my initial assumptions.

Experimenting with Whisking Technique

Action Application

Adjusting whisk speed, rhythm, and wrist motion altered foam structure and mouthfeel. This demonstrated that preparation is not neutral, but actively shapes the final expression of quality.

Testing Water Temperature

Action Analysis

Lower water temperatures enhanced sweetness and reduced bitterness, while higher temperatures intensified astringency. This revealed how extraction conditions influence chemical perception.

Tea Ceremony Experience

Observation Cultural Insight

Participating in a formal tea ceremony reframed matcha quality as something inseparable from ritual, intention, and environment. The experience emphasized harmony and presence over isolated sensory traits.

Shift in Understanding of Quality

Evaluation Synthesis

Quality became less about measurable attributes and more about balance, context, and experience. This marked a shift from a technical definition of quality to a more holistic and culturally embedded understanding.
