During

This phase translates the matcha lifecycle system into lived experience. Through tasting, preparation, and observation, each layer reflects how interaction reshapes the perception of quality.

Encounter
Application
Perception
Comparative Tasting Tasting multiple matcha side by side revealed differences in bitterness, aroma, and color, making grading distinctions more tangible.
Variation Across Samples Even within similar grades, subtle differences emerged, suggesting that quality exists along a spectrum rather than fixed categories.
Whisking Technique Adjusting whisking speed and motion changed foam structure and texture, demonstrating how preparation directly affects experience.
Water Temperature Lower temperatures enhanced sweetness and reduced bitterness, revealing the importance of extraction control.
Redefining Quality Quality became less about fixed traits and more about balance, intention, and context.
Role of Context The same matcha felt different depending on environment and preparation, suggesting quality is partially constructed through experience.
