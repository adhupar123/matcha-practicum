Cultivar (品種) Genetic lineage regulates chlorophyll density, amino acid synthesis, and catechin balance. Shade-adapted cultivars retain elevated L-theanine levels, producing enhanced sweetness and refined bitterness structure.
Soil Composition (土壌) Nitrogen availability increases amino acid production. Balanced mineral ecosystems improve nutrient uptake efficiency and sweetness persistence.
Harvest Timing (一番茶) First flush leaves exhibit maximal L-theanine concentration and minimal fiber, leading to smoother texture and reduced astringency.
Shading Strategy (覆下栽培) Pre-harvest shading suppresses catechin formation, deepening umami and preserving jade coloration.
Processing (製茶工程) Immediate steaming halts enzymatic oxidation, preserving chlorophyll and preventing bitterness escalation.
Stone Milling (石臼挽き) Slow granite milling prevents thermal degradation, preserving aromatics and particle integrity.
Freshness Management (鮮度管理) Cold storage slows oxidation, maintaining sweetness clarity and vibrant color.
Water Character (湯質) Soft water enhances sweetness perception, while mineral hardness sharpens catechin structure.
Color (色) High chlorophyll concentration produces deep jade vibrancy indicating preserved integrity.
Aroma (香り) Volatile grassy aldehydes define freshness and marine sweetness.
Umami (旨味) L-theanine stimulates glutamate receptors, creating depth and lingering sweetness.
Bitterness (渋み) Catechin balance determines structural tension and complexity.
Texture (質感) Ultra-fine particles create a stable suspension and ceremonial creaminess.
