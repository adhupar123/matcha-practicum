The Matcha Lifecycle System

Biological potential defines possibility. Craft refines it. Sensory perception reveals it. Scroll to move through the layers.

Cultivar (品種) Genetics determine amino acid and chlorophyll capacity.
Soil Composition (土壌) Mineral density influences sweetness and structural complexity.
Harvest Timing (一番茶) First flush maximizes L-theanine and reduces fiber.
Shading Strategy (覆下栽培) Pre-harvest shading intensifies umami and preserves chlorophyll.
Processing (製茶工程) Steaming halts oxidation; drying stabilizes color.
Stone Milling (石臼挽き) Slow grinding creates ultra-fine particles.
Freshness Management (鮮度管理) Cold storage preserves aroma and vibrancy.
Water Character (湯質) Mineral content and temperature influence bitterness expression.
Color (色) Vivid jade hue signals chlorophyll preservation.
Aroma (香り) Volatile compounds define freshness.
Umami (旨味) Driven by L-theanine concentration.
Bitterness (渋み) Catechins determine astringency.
Texture (質感) Microfoam and fine particle size create ceremonial creaminess.
