The Matcha Lifecycle System

Scroll to move through biological foundations, craft transformation, and sensory expression. The diagram remains fixed while influence layers are emphasized.

Cultivar (品種) Genetic lineage regulates L-theanine synthesis, chlorophyll accumulation, and catechin expression. Shade-adapted cultivars maintain higher amino acid retention, producing enhanced sweetness, greater umami depth, and reduced harsh phenolic bitterness.
Soil Composition (土壌) Nitrogen availability directly increases amino acid production within the leaf. Balanced mineral ecosystems improve root absorption efficiency, resulting in structural complexity and greater sweetness persistence in the cup.
Harvest Timing (一番茶) First flush leaves exhibit maximal L-theanine and minimal cellulose fiber. Early harvesting preserves tenderness and vibrancy, leading to smoother texture and diminished astringency.
Shading Strategy (覆下栽培) Pre-harvest shading suppresses photosynthesis, preventing conversion of L-theanine into catechins. This preserves chlorophyll density and amplifies umami concentration.
Processing (製茶工程) Steaming denatures oxidative enzymes immediately after harvest. This prevents polyphenol oxidation, preserving vibrant color and preventing bitterness escalation.
Stone Milling (石臼挽き) Granite milling at low rotational speed prevents thermal degradation. Maintaining low heat preserves aromatic compounds and structural integrity.
Freshness Management (鮮度管理) Oxygen exposure oxidizes chlorophyll and volatile aromatics. Cold storage slows degradation, preserving sweetness clarity and color brightness.
Water Character (湯質) Mineral hardness interacts with catechins and amino acids. Soft water enhances sweetness perception, while hard water increases astringency and structural sharpness.
Color (色) Chlorophyll concentration determines vibrancy. High-quality matcha displays deep jade coloration, indicating preserved leaf integrity.
Aroma (香り) Volatile grassy aldehydes and marine-like notes define freshness. These compounds diminish rapidly with oxidation.
Umami (旨味) L-theanine concentration stimulates glutamate receptors, creating depth and lingering sweetness.
Bitterness (渋み) Catechin density determines structural tension. Balanced shading and harvest timing moderate harsh phenolic expression.
Texture (質感) Ultra-fine 5–10 micron particles form a stable colloidal suspension. Proper whisking generates microfoam, enhancing viscosity and perceived creaminess.
