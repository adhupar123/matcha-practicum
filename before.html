Matcha Anatomy

Mapping cultivation variables to sensory quality and biological mechanisms. Hover over each trait to explore its structure.

Color
Mechanism: Increased chlorophyll under 3–4 weeks shading.

Impact: Vivid green signals high amino acid retention and careful oxidation control.
Umami
Mechanism: L-theanine preserved through reduced sunlight conversion.

Impact: Produces smooth, savory depth valued in ceremonial grade matcha.
Bitterness
Mechanism: Catechin concentration increases with sunlight and maturity.

Impact: Higher bitterness often indicates lower ceremonial quality.
Texture
Mechanism: Stone grinding reduces particles to ~10µm.

Impact: Fine suspension produces smooth, creamy mouthfeel.
Aroma
Mechanism: Preservation of volatile organic compounds.

Impact: Fresh milling enhances sweet, marine, grassy notes.
